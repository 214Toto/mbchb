INTRODUCTION TO PATHOLOGY

Define Pathology
✓ scientific study of the molecular, cellular, tissue, or organ system response to injurious agents or adverse influences
Why do you one should study Pathology ?

✓ for The function of a clinician is to diagnose and treat the disease
✓ A clinician cannot rationally diagnose and treat without understanding the disease process with which he/she is dealing with
✓ pathology is the ‘corner-stone’ of medicine

Define Health
✓ " refers to the "state in which an individual is living in complete harmony with his/her environment," it is a relative state.
Define Disease
✓ A disease may be defined as a "state in which an individual exhibits an anatomical, physiological, or biochemical deviation from the normal."
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe General Pathology.
✓ Refers to the study of the basic alterations in tissues.
✓ These are changes that apply to most of the organs or tissues of the body and include such things as atrophy, necrosis and inflammation.
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe Systemic Pathology.
✓ Refers to the study of the diseases of the organ systems of the body such as the respiratory system, digestive system and nervous system. 
✓ It is the application of knowledge of general pathology in order to study specific diseases, organ by organ.
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe Gross pathology.
✓ Refers to the study of disease in which tissues and organs are examined with the unaided eye.
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe Cellular pathology.
✓ Refers to the study of diseased tissues and organs with the aid of a microscope
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe Surgical pathology.
✓ Refers to the study of tissues removed at the time of surgery. 
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe Clinical pathology (Laboratory Medicine).
✓ Refers to the study of disease by examination of blood, urine, faeces, skin scrapings, etc.
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe Immunopathology.
✓ Refers to the study of diseases associated with abnormalities of the immune mechanisms of the body.
Regarding the Branches of Pathology, Describe Chemical pathology.
✓ The study of chemical alterations in body fluids and tissues that result from disease e.g. the analysis of liver enzymes (ALT, AST) in hepatitis.
Describe the two main categories of diseases.
✓ Acute Diseases ⊳ Sudden Onset & Short duration
✓ Chronic Diseases ⊳ insidious onset and protracted course
While giving an example, list the type of agents causing diseases.
✓ Infectious organisms => Virus
✓ Physical => Trauma
✓ Chemical => toxic organic substances
✓ Nutritional => deficieny of vitamins
✓ Genetic Defects => Cystic Fibrosis mutation in the CFTR gene
Define a Lesion
✓ structural or morphological alterations associated with a diseased state in an individual
Define a Pathognomonic Lesion
✓ it : is a change that is specifically characteristic of a disease
Define a Clinical sign.
✓ refer to any "functional evidence of disease that can be determined objectively by the observer" e.g. lameness, salivation, increased respiratory efforts, etc. (clinical signs are seen only in the living individual).
Define clinical symptoms
✓ functional evidence of disease that can be determined subjectively by the patient" e.g., feeling of abdominal discomfort
Define a syndrome
✓ refers to a group of symptoms and other changes in the body’s functions which, when taken together, show that a particular disease is present.
Define prognosis.
✓ probable outcome of a disease in a living individual
✓ Outcomes include: Complete recovery, Partial healing or Death
Define Diagnosis
✓ determination of the nature of a disease expressed in a concise manner.
Describe the different types of Diagnosis.
✓ morphological or anatomical diagnosis is based on the location and nature of the lesion
✓ Aetiologic diagnosis is made on the basis of the cause
✓ Definitive diagnosis is made on the basis of the specific disease entity involved
✓ A clinical diagnosis is made on the basis of clinical signs observed in the living individual
Define autopsy
✓ Refers to gross examination of the cadaver by systematic dissection in order to evaluate any abnormal changes (lesions) that may be present.
Define Biopsy
✓ refers to the removal and examination of tissue obtained from the living body.
Define Somatic death
✓ refers to death of the entire body; there is cessation of all body functions
Define Postmortem changes
✓ refer to cell death which accompanies or occurs after death of the entire body (somatic death).
Define Ante mortem changes
✓ refer to those alterations that occur in cells, tissues, organs, etc. prior to somatic death or in the living individual
define Post mortem autolysis
✓ refers to self-digestion by enzymes that are present within or released into the cytoplasm of cells after death. It is due to total diffuse anoxia.
Define Post mortem putrefaction (rotting)
✓ refers to the decomposition of tissues by bacterial enzymes after death of the entire body
Define Rigor mortis
✓ refers to stiffening of all muscles after death. It is related to a progressive decrease in oxygen, ATP, creatinine phosphate, and pH of muscles
Define Livor mortis
✓ refers to the discolouration of dependent parts of the body after death as a result of red blood cell destruction. It is seen as the “gravitational settling of blood” in the lower (dependent) parts of the body after death.
Define Algor mortis
✓ refers to the cooling of the body after death.
